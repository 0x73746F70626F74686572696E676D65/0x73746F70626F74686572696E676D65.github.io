I want to talk a bit about how I approach a target when attempting to "hack" it. Now I put hack in quotes because hack is a very vague term. Googles definition at the 
moment states: "use a computer to gain unauthorized access to data in a system", and Wikipedia states: "a hacker is a person skilled in information technology who uses
their technical knowledge to achieve a goal or overcome an obstacle, within a computerized system by non-standard means". Clearly Wikipedia takes the cake here 
because it's more vague. It's good to be vague when building a methodology because it can be re-applied to any subfield of a field. I noticed most of hacking comes down
to a couple of ideas. 

Working from end to beginning. Find your ideal end goal for the target you're trying to hack, and then ask yourself "how do I make that possible?". Another obvious idea
is believing that the impossible is possible in your scenario. If you don't believe that your end goal is possible, then you've already failed. Unless you're bottlenecked 
by physics, anything is possible. One extreme example could be: "Bitcoin is unhackable, it's cryptographically secure". Well, depending on your end goal, Bitcoin can be
"hacked". It has been done before. Not by attacking the
cryptography, but by attacking the codebase (not to take away from the fact that cryptography can be hacked as well). My point is, there's always a way to do 
something. The real question is how far are you willing to go to achieve it. If your end goal was to take Bitcoin completely offline, you could just nuke the entire 
planet, find a networking DOS exploit that affects the entire internet, find a bug in Bitcoins codebase that causes a DOS, or anything you fucking wish to do. You want 
to find your end goal, and then imagine the most efficient/realistic way to achieve that goal, and become obsessed about it. When you encounter scenarios where you are 
uneducated on a certain field of knowledge that is necessary to achieve your end goal, simply google/research the fuck out of it.

It's like this never ending loop of research, theory, discovery, and application. Download all the knowledge necessary to understand the target into your brain.
Theorize on how you want your exploit look, search for vulnerabilities that help you achive that theoretical final exploit, and execute that bitch. 


Remember to take everything I just said with a grain of salt. This is just my way of approaching "hacking" and I'm severely flawed as a person. This is solely a template of ideas that could be of use to anyone looking to further understand the concept of hacking.
